Why Your 'Data Story' Matters

The Company's Need

Companies don't hire for technical skills alone. They hire for problem-solvers.

→

The Data-Business Gap

Many businesses struggle to see how data activities translate into tangible outcomes (revenue, cost savings, etc.).

→

Your Solution: The Pitch

A powerful pitch bridges this gap by framing your skills as a means to create business impact.

This is how you stand out.

The 3-Part Pitch Framework

1

The Context (The "Before")

Briefly state your background or the business problem.

"In my previous role at a retail company, we struggled with understanding customer churn..."

2

The Action (Your Part)

Describe what **you** did. Mention 1-2 key data skills/tools.

"...so I used Python to analyze transaction data and built a predictive model to identify at-risk customers."

3

The Impact (The "So What?")

This is crucial! Quantify the result. How did you help the business?

"...This reduced churn by 15% in Q1, saving an estimated £200,000."

Key Takeaways & Confidence Boosters

  • ✓
    Lead with Impact: Always connect your work back to a business outcome (Revenue, Cost, etc.).
  • ✓
    Quantify Everything: Use numbers, percentages, and monetary values. They make your contribution undeniable.
  • ✓
    Know Your Audience: Tailor your pitch. A technical manager wants model details; a business leader wants the bottom line.
  • ✓
    Practice Makes Perfect: Your pitch is a living story. Refine it continuously. Confidence comes from preparation.
